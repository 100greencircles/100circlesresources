For Founders Who Already Know This

You've started.
But something's not working.

You're not new to the idea of video. You've thought about it, tried it, maybe even posted. But consistent, high-quality, on-brand video that actually builds your presence? That part hasn't clicked yet.

01

"I post occasionally but there's no real system"

Your output is reactive and inconsistent — a burst of posts, then silence for weeks. No framework, no cadence, no clarity on what to make next. Video feels like something you do when you have time, not a presence you're building.

02

"The quality doesn't match who I am"

Your ideas are sharp. Your thinking is strong. But the videos don't reflect that. The camera flattens something. The delivery doesn't land the way it does in a room. You know you're capable of better — you just don't know how to get there.

03

"It takes too long and the return feels unclear"

When you do sit down to record, it takes hours. The time cost doesn't feel justified for the result. And you're not sure if video is actually moving the needle on anything that matters — leads, trust, visibility, talent.

04

"I know what to say — just not how to say it on camera"

In a room, you command attention. On screen, something shifts — the energy drops, the words come out differently. Nobody's told you exactly what to fix, and you're too busy building a company to figure it out alone.

05

"My founder story and POV aren't clearly defined yet"

You have a strong point of view — but it hasn't translated into a consistent video identity. Viewers can't tell from your content what you stand for, who you serve, or why your perspective is worth following.

06

"The inertia is real — I keep pushing it to next week"

Video creation keeps getting deprioritised. Not because it's unimportant — you know it is. But without a forcing function, it never rises above the day-to-day. The gap between intention and execution keeps growing.

The gap isn't talent or ideas. It's consistency, system, time — and the inertia that kills all three.

The founders building strong video presence aren't more talented. They've solved the infrastructure problem — a framework for what to say, a system for how to show up, and the reps to break through the inertia. That's exactly what this weekend is designed to give you.
