Key Insights & Observations
What the Data Tells Us
🚀
YouTube is the Primary View Engine
YouTube drove 58.6% of all views and 66% of all watch hours (54.7 hrs) despite the ad adjustment. With an avg of ~540 views/video, it was 10× more efficient per video than LinkedIn and 1.6× more than Instagram.
⭐
One Video Carried Instagram
"This CEO is more famous than his own company" (Day 14) generated 3,482 views — over 35% of Instagram's entire 30-day total alone. Without it, Instagram's average would drop from 335 to ~216 views/video.
💼
LinkedIn Punches Above Its Weight in Engagement Rate
LinkedIn's 6.7% engagement rate (103 engagements on 1,562 views) is exceptional for any platform. Compared to Instagram at ~2.6%, LinkedIn's audience — while smaller — is significantly more engaged and action-oriented.
📺
YouTube Retention & CTR Signal Quality Audience
With an average CTR of 2.8% and 34.5% viewer retention, the channel's short-form content is resonating. The best performers — Bajaj Finance (61% retention, 6.3% CTR) — show what's possible when the hook and topic align perfectly.
📉
LinkedIn Fatigue in the Back Half
LinkedIn views dropped from an avg of 71 (Days 1–10) to just 35 (Days 21–30) — a 51% decline. This may reflect algorithm de-prioritisation of the same account posting daily, or audience attention saturation on LinkedIn specifically.
🎯
CEO / Brand Storytelling = Viral Formula
The top Instagram performers were CEO stories (famous CEO, Red Bull, Bajaj Finance) and brand case studies (Slack, Intercom, HubSpot). Human + brand narratives consistently outperformed tactical or how-to content on Instagram.
⚡
AI & Tactical Content Wins on YouTube
YouTube's top 5 by views were dominated by actionable, AI-related, or platform-growth content — ChatGPT CRM, LinkedIn hacks, and SaaS case studies. Titles with a clear "how" or "why X is big" structure performed best.
📊
Watch Hours: Instagram Outperforms at Scale
Instagram generated 21.67 watch hours from just 9,859 views vs LinkedIn's 6.38 hrs from 1,562 views. The Red Bull video alone drove ~289 minutes — nearly 5 hours. Viral content exponentially amplifies watch time.
🔄
Cross-Platform Consistency is Rare but Powerful
Only 2–3 videos ranked in the top 5 on more than one platform. "This CEO is more famous…" and "LinkedIn hack to turn strangers into customers" are the most versatile formats — suggesting persona + tactical hook as the ideal content formula.
📋 Campaign Summary
What Worked
✅ CEO & brand storytelling — dominated Instagram and crossed over to LinkedIn
✅ AI/productivity hooks — best YouTube performers used AI angles
✅ SaaS case studies — Intercom, HubSpot, Slack drove strong YT watch time
✅ Question-based titles — "Why Do It?", "Costing You Business?" drove curiosity
✅ LinkedIn audience quality — 6.7% engagement rate is world-class
Opportunities
💡 LinkedIn consistency — volumes declined 50% by end; posting cadence or algo rotation may help
💡 Replicate viral format — CEO + brand origin story clearly works; double down on this
💡 YouTube CTR optimization — avg 2.8% CTR; thumbnail/title testing could unlock more volume
💡 Instagram reach diversification — 90%+ of reach came from just 2 videos; build a more consistent base
